AI Founders Are About to Get Sherlocked — and Most Don't See It Coming

Matt Rogers co-founded Nest, worked directly under Steve Jobs, and watched Apple kill startup after startup with a single OS update. He's sounding the alarm for AI founders: the same pattern is repeating right now. The companies that survive won't have the best models — they'll be the ones that become operationally indispensable.

Key Takeaways

  • Apple's "Sherlocking" — absorbing startups into the OS overnight — is now being replicated by OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google with every major model release.
  • Products built as model wrappers or generic copilots are the most vulnerable. Small enough to bundle, no network effects to protect them.
  • Dropbox, Spotify, and 1Password all survived platform attacks by going deeper — becoming embedded in operations rather than competing feature-for-feature.
  • The frontier labs can build extraordinary foundation models — but they can't provide white-glove implementation for every hospital, law firm, and manufacturer on earth. That gap is the real opportunity.
  • The moat is operational integration: approvals, compliance, procurement, institutional knowledge. Once you're embedded, you're painful to remove.
By Matt Rogers (Co-founder, Nest & Mill)  ·  Fortune  ·  May 30, 2026

Why Databricks Is Worth $134 Billion — And What It Tells Us About the AI Infrastructure Race

Databricks just closed a $7B+ funding round at a $134 billion valuation — one of the largest in private company history — and is heading toward what may be the defining IPO of the AI era. The reason? It solves the problem every enterprise faces but rarely talks about: their data is a mess, and AI is completely useless without clean, accessible data.

Why This Matters

  • Databricks built the "data lakehouse" — a unified platform that makes enterprise data actually usable for AI. No clean data, no working AI. That's the bottleneck every large company hits.
  • $5.4B ARR growing 65% year-over-year. In a market full of hype, those are real numbers earned from real operational dependency.
  • IPO expected second half of 2026 — likely to be a benchmark valuation event for the entire AI infrastructure sector.
  • This is a textbook case of the Sherlocking survival playbook in action: don't compete with the models, become the essential layer underneath them.
  • Every enterprise that signs with Databricks is essentially locked in — their data architecture, pipelines, and workflows all run through it. That's the moat.
By David H. Freedman  ·  Inc.  ·  May 2026

🎸 Dupree's Take

Read these two stories together and a clear picture emerges: the AI winners of this decade won't be the ones with the best models — they'll be the ones who own the layer that everything else runs on top of. Databricks owns the data layer. The companies Rogers describes own the workflow layer. The frontier labs own the intelligence layer. The question for every AI builder right now is: which layer are you in, and how deep can you go?
