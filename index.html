AI Founders Are About to Get Sherlocked — and Most Don't See It Coming

Matt Rogers co-founded Nest, worked directly under Steve Jobs, and watched Apple kill startup after startup with a single OS update. He's now sounding the alarm for AI founders: the same thing is happening again, right now. The companies that survive won't be the ones with the best models — they'll be the ones that become operationally indispensable.

Key Takeaways

  • Apple's "Sherlocking" — absorbing startups into the OS overnight — is now being replicated by OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google with every model release.
  • Products built as "model wrappers" or generic copilots are the most vulnerable. They're small enough to bundle and lack the network effects to survive.
  • Dropbox, Spotify, and 1Password all survived platform attacks by going deeper — not by competing feature-for-feature, but by becoming embedded in operations.
  • The frontier labs can build extraordinary foundation models — but they can't provide white-glove implementation for every hospital, law firm, and manufacturer on earth. That gap is the opportunity.
  • The moat is operational integration: approvals, compliance, procurement, institutional knowledge. Once you're in, you're painful to remove.
By Matt Rogers (Co-founder, Nest & Mill)  ·  Fortune  ·  May 30, 2026
Read Full Story
🎸 Dupree's Take

Rogers is describing exactly why personal AI is the most defensible position in this entire market. A Muse that knows your life, your relationships, your history, and your preferences cannot be "Sherlocked" — because it can't be replicated at scale. The hyperscalers build wide. We build deep. That asymmetry is the whole game.
